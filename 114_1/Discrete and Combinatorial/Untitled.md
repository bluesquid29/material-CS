The Division Algorithm

> [!note] The Division Algorithm
> Let $a \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $m \in \mathbb{Z}^{+}$
> Then there exist unique integers $q, r \in \mathbb{Z}$
> $$
> \large a = m \cdot q  + r, \quad 0 \leq r < m
> $$

> [!note] Theorem
> Let $a, b \in \mathbb{Z}$, and $m \in \mathbb{Z}^{+}$ 
> Then
> $$
> \begin{align}
> a \equiv b \, (\text{mod}\,m) &\iff a \,\text{mod}\,m = b \,\text{mod}\,m \\
> &\iff a \text{ and } b \text{ have the same remainder when divided by } m
> \end{align}
> $$
